The intelligence layer

AI makes the difference between
opinion and fact immediate.

For the first time, the gap between what people believe and what science actually says can be closed in real time — at scale, automatically, every single morning.

The problem it solves
Opinion travels at the speed of social media. Fact used to travel at the speed of a research department.
Before AI, cross-referencing a trending health claim against peer-reviewed literature required hours of researcher time — making real-time fact-checking economically impossible at scale. A single viral claim could reach 2 million people before any correction existed. That asymmetry is what Yummy Wellness is built to collapse.
Sourcing opinion
What people actually believe
Reddit communities, global news feeds, and social platforms surface what millions of people are genuinely discussing, sharing, and acting on — in real time, across 65 countries.
Sourcing fact
What the science actually says
Claude AI cross-references every claim against BMJ, Lancet, JAMA, CDC, WHO, NHS, and peer-reviewed nutrition journals — in seconds. The same task took a research team hours in 2020.
Global context
How culture shapes what we believe
The same claim scores differently in Germany, Japan, France, and the US — because regulatory frameworks, culinary traditions, and health authority positions genuinely diverge. AI surfaces that nuance instantly.
The result
Opinion and fact, side by side, every morning — automatically.
For the first time, the machinery exists to close the gap between what people believe and what science says — not occasionally, not with a team of researchers, but daily and automatically. Yummy Wellness is the interface that makes that intelligence accessible, warm, and actionable for anyone who eats.
